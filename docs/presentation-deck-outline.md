# Presentation Deck Outline: Basecamp Rewards Refresh
**Audience:** Dana (Marketing Director), CFO
**Length:** 15 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A
**Goal:** Get $28,000 approved to launch the Coffee Personality Quiz

---

## Slide 1 — Title
**Headline:** Turning Around Basecamp Rewards: A 90-Day Plan
**Subhead:** From "fine but forgettable" to a program people actually love
**Visual:** Warm cream background, Basecamp logo, quiz URL as a subtle footer

*Speaker note: Don't spend time here. Move fast to the problem.*

---

## Slide 2 — The Honest Picture
**Headline:** The program is losing money and losing members
**Content:**

| Metric | Today |
|--------|-------|
| Monthly active users | 2,100 (target: 8,000) |
| Avg visits/month | 1.6 (target: 4.0) |
| 30-day retention | 27% (target: 60%) |
| Program ROI | 0.5x |
| Program NPS | 12 |

**Visual:** Red/amber trend arrows on each metric

*Speaker note: Don't apologize for the numbers. Own them. The CFO already knows it's bad — show you do too.*

---

## Slide 3 — Why It's Failing
**Headline:** People love Basecamp. They don't care about the rewards.
**Content:**
- Brand NPS: **67** ← people love us
- Program NPS: **12** ← they don't love this

> *"Your coffee has personality. Your rewards program doesn't. Fix that and you'd have something."*
> — Basecamp member, March 2026

**Visual:** Two NPS gauges side by side — 67 vs. 12

*Speaker note: This is the key insight. The brand is strong. The program just isn't connected to it. That's actually good news — we're not fixing the brand, we're fixing the program.*

---

## Slide 4 — The Root Cause
**Headline:** Every campaign was built on a wrong premise
**Content:**
- 10 campaigns over 6 months
- Every single one: more points, double points, bonus points
- Result: temporary spikes, no sustained behavior change

> *"I don't feel like a valued customer. I feel like a transaction."*
> — Basecamp member feedback

**Visual:** Simple timeline of 10 campaigns, all flat after each spike

*Speaker note: This isn't a criticism of the previous team. It's the industry default. Everyone does points programs. That's exactly why this is an opportunity.*

---

## Slide 5 — The Opportunity
**Headline:** No competitor is doing coffee identity at scale
**Content:**
- Starbucks: personalizes based on what you already order (reflects the past, not you)
- Dutch Bros: builds relationships through baristas (can't scale past 5 locations)
- **Nobody has given customers a language for their coffee identity**

That's the white space. And customers are already asking for it:
> *"I wish you could help me find new drinks to try."*
> *"What if you helped people find their signature drink?"*

*Speaker note: This is where you lean in. Genuine competitive white space is rare. We have it.*

---

## Slide 6 — The Solution (Demo)
**Headline:** The Coffee Personality Quiz
**Content:** Live demo — open https://quiz-project-xi-livid.vercel.app on screen

Walk through:
1. Intro screen
2. One or two questions
3. Jump to results — show a personality card

**Key points to hit:**
- 6 questions, ~60 seconds
- 4 personalities: Zen Minimalist, Sweet Enthusiast, Wellness Explorer, Practical Pragmatist
- Each maps to a drink recommendation
- Results are shareable — organic acquisition
- Prototype is already built and deployed

*Speaker note: Let the demo breathe. Don't talk over it. If Dana or the CFO wants to take the full quiz, let them. That's a good sign.*

---

**Content:**

| Shareable results | Organic acquisition — free growth |
