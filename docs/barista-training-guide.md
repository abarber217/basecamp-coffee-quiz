# Barista Training Guide: Coffee Personality Quiz
**For:** All Basecamp Coffee locations
**Prepared by:** Loyalty Program Manager
**Date:** March 2026

---

## Hey there, Basecamp crew.

You know that question you get asked every single day? "What should I try?"

People ask because they trust you. And up until now, the app has never been able to help with that — it just tracked points. That changes now.

We built a **Coffee Personality Quiz** in the Basecamp Rewards app. It takes about a minute, gives members a coffee identity, and recommends a drink that actually matches who they are. Think of it as doing digitally what you do at the counter every day — helping people find their thing.

This guide tells you everything you need to know to talk about it naturally.

---

## The Four Personalities

When someone takes the quiz, they get one of four results. Know these — members will want to talk about them.

| Personality | Vibe | Their Drink |
|-------------|------|-------------|
| ☕ **The Zen Minimalist** | Slow mornings, intentional, asks about the beans | Single-Origin Pour-Over |
| 🍮 **The Sweet Enthusiast** | Loves seasonal specials, indulgent, warm energy | Vanilla Latte |
| 🍵 **The Wellness Explorer** | Oat milk, mindful, always curious | Matcha Oat Latte |
| ⚡ **The Practical Pragmatist** | Needs caffeine, no fuss, reliable | Cold Brew |

You'll probably recognize your regulars in these immediately.

---

## When to Bring It Up

You don't need to pitch it to everyone. Look for these natural moments:

**Someone asks "what should I try?"**
This is the perfect opening. The quiz was literally built for this question.

**A member scans their rewards and mentions the app**
Easy transition — they're already in the app mindset.

**Someone is waiting and seems open to chat**
If the line is clear and the vibe is right, mention it casually.

**A new member signs up in-store**
Mention it as part of the welcome: "While you've got the app open, there's a quick quiz that helps figure out your coffee style."

---

## What to Say

These are conversation starters, not scripts. Use your own words — that's the whole point.

**The short version (10 seconds):**
> "Hey, have you tried the coffee personality quiz in the app? Takes about a minute and it's actually pretty spot-on."

**If they seem curious:**
> "It asks you six questions — nothing serious, just stuff like how you take your coffee and what your mornings look like — and it tells you what kind of coffee person you are. I'm a [your result], which tracks."

**If they just got their result:**
> "Oh nice, what did you get?" *(then riff on it — this is the fun part)*

**If they haven't downloaded the app yet:**
> "It's in the Basecamp Rewards app. If you grab it, the quiz is right on the home screen."

---

## Handling Common Questions

**"What's the point of the quiz?"**
> "It helps the app actually get to know you — so instead of just tracking points, it can recommend drinks you'd actually like. Personalized stuff."

**"Is it just a marketing thing?"**
> "Honestly? It's pretty fun. And the recommendations are legit — it's not just suggesting whatever we're pushing that week."

**"I already know what I like."**
> "Fair. It's more about discovering things you might not have tried. A lot of people end up surprised by their result."

**"I don't have the app."**
> "You can take it at [quiz URL] too — no app needed." *(quiz-project-xi-livid.vercel.app — or check the counter card for the QR code)*

**"What did you get?"**
Take the quiz before your first shift with this guide. Know your result. This is the easiest way to make it feel real.

---

## After They Take It

If a member shares their result with you, lean into it. This is where it gets fun.

- **Zen Minimalist?** Ask them if they've tried whatever single-origin we're featuring this week.
- **Sweet Enthusiast?** Tell them about the seasonal special.
- **Wellness Explorer?** Point them to any oat milk or lower-sugar options.
- **Practical Pragmatist?** Respect the cold brew. Maybe mention the new batch if it's fresh.

You already do this intuitively. The quiz just gives it a language.

---

## What You Don't Need to Do

- You don't need to hard-sell it. Mention it once, naturally. If they're not interested, move on.
- You don't need to memorize anything. Just know the four personalities and the vibe of each.
- You don't need to troubleshoot app issues. If something's broken, tell them to reach out to us via the app's help section.

---

## Take the Quiz First

Seriously — before you start talking about it, take it yourself. It takes 60 seconds and you'll have a much easier time explaining it once you've actually done it.

Then find a coworker and compare results. That's what members are going to do.
